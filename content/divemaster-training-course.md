## 1. Purpose & Philosophy
The Dive India Divemaster Training Course is designed to produce world-class dive professionals who are confident, competent, environmentally responsible, and industry-ready. This program combines Scuba Schools International (SSI) training standards with Dive India’s unique operational environment and philosophy, offering candidates a blend of structured academic instruction, real-world dive center experience, and professional development.

---

## 2. Prerequisites
- **Minimum age:** 18 years  
- **Swimming ability:** Ability to swim 400 m and float/tread water for 10 minutes  
- **Medical:** Fit to dive (medical clearance within the last 12 months)  
- **Commitment:** Willingness to commit to a full-time internship for the agreed duration (2–6 months based on entry-level certification and experience)  
- **Agreement:** Read and signed Candidate–Dive Center Agreement (Appendix A)

---

## 3. Course Structure & Phases

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Orientation & Prep | 2 weeks | Dive center integration, safety orientation, gear setup, swim tests, OWD (if required) |
| Academic Training | 4 weeks (spread out) | Navigation, Night, Deep, React Right, SSI Dive Guide + Science of Diving theory, reviews, exams |
| Water Skills & Rescue | 4 weeks | Skill mastery, rescue drills (responsive/unresponsive divers), stamina tests, skill circuit |
| Guided Assists & Leading Dives | 8 weeks | Assisting instructors, guiding certified divers, site briefings, office & boat logistics |
| Workshops & Eco Modules | 4 weeks | Compressor ops, basic servicing, marine ecology, photography/video workshops |
| Professional Prep & Assessment | 2 weeks | Mock scenarios, final assessments, career readiness |

---

## 4. Weekly Expectations
- **Internship days:** 5–6 days/week  
- Participation in:
  - Daily dive operations  
  - Equipment cleaning, storage, and logistics  
  - Staff meetings (“Bubble Briefs”)  
  - Weekly mentor check-ins  
- Arrive **at least 30 minutes** before your scheduled reporting time  
- Keep equipment ready, personal dive log up to date, and follow the day’s dive plan

---

## 5. Core Training Modules

### A. Orientation
- Dive Center safety procedures: oxygen kit locations & usage, fire safety, emergency contacts  
- Roles within the dive center (front desk, equipment, boats, instructors, guides)  
- Intern handbook briefing

### B. Academic Learning
- SSI Dive Guide & Science of Diving digital learning modules  
- Weekly classroom sessions  
- Open-book quizzes and final exams  
- Theory review and mentoring

### C. In-Water Training
- Skill Circuit (24 skills, demo-quality execution)  
- Rescue scenarios (responsive/unresponsive divers)  
- Swim & stamina tests (400 m swim, 800 m snorkel, 15-minute tread)  
- Specialty dives: navigation, deep, night, limited visibility

### D. Dive Leading
- Site briefings: maps, marine life, emergency plans  
- Dive planning & logistics  
- Guest handling & safety management  
- Mapping project & dive planning assessments

### E. Workshops
- Compressor operation & tank handling  
- Basic gear servicing & maintenance logs  
- Customer service & bookings  
- Boat handling & mooring line use  
- Marine ecology (SSI Specialty or internal module)

---

## 6. Professional Standards
Candidates must:
- Maintain a professional appearance (clean, neat, Dive India uniform where applicable)  
- Use clear, respectful communication with staff, guests, and other interns  
- Refrain from language or behaviour that is offensive, discriminatory, or unprofessional  
- Show punctuality, reliability, and readiness to take initiative  
- Model environmental responsibility — avoid touching marine life, secure gear to prevent reef damage

---

## 7. Responsibilities of Interns
- Maintain all assigned equipment, storerooms, and common areas in clean, serviceable condition  
- Log all dives and skills daily  
- Follow daily routines and attend staff briefings  
- Shadow instructors and guides, gradually taking leadership roles when signed off  
- Assist in office, dock, or logistics tasks as assigned

---

## 8. Instructor & Dive Center Responsibilities
- Provide required training, supervision, and mentorship  
- Ensure access to learning materials, equipment, and facilities  
- Offer regular, constructive feedback  
- Engage candidates in real dive operations to develop professional competence  
- Foster an inclusive, respectful learning environment

---

## 9. Evaluation & Completion Criteria
- Weekly feedback sessions with mentor instructors  
- Progress tracking in skills, academics, and professional conduct  
- Minimum **60 logged dives** by completion  
- Certification requirements:
  - SSI Dive Guide  
  - SSI Science of Diving  
- Internal Dive India final evaluation (all modules signed off)  
- On successful completion:
  - SSI Divemaster certification (issued by Dive India)  
  - Certificate of Internship Completion  
  - Optional Letter of Recommendation for exceptional performance

---

## 10. Disciplinary Process
- Grounds for dismissal:
  - Unsafe, unethical, or unprofessional behaviour  
  - Repeated breaches of conduct or poor participation  
  - Violation of Dive India policies or SSI safety standards  
- Process:
  1. Verbal warning with corrective guidance  
  2. Written warning with improvement plan  
  3. Termination of internship

---

## 11. Exit Protocol
- Final feedback session (candidate + mentor + manager)  
- Completion of exit checklist:
  - Return of all Dive India equipment  
  - Clearance of accounts/documents  
  - Final logbook and training record review  
- Career guidance & next-steps discussion (Instructor Training Course, job search)

## Appendices

### Appendix A – Candidate–Dive Center Agreement
1. **Commitment:** Full participation in all training, dives, and assignments.  
2. **Behaviour:** Maintain professionalism, punctuality, and positive attitude.  
3. **Compliance:** Follow Dive India SOPs, safety standards, and SSI protocols.  
4. **Respect:** Treat all staff, interns, and guests with courtesy.  
5. **Records:** Maintain logs, academic work, and skill sign-offs in a timely manner.  
6. **Consequences:** Dive India reserves the right to terminate the internship for unsafe, unethical, or unprofessional conduct.
