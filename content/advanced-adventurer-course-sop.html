1. Purpose & Scope

This SOP supplements the SSI standards for Advanced Adventurer / Advanced Open Water training. It defines Dive India’s operational standards for delivering up to five themed adventure dives, ensuring safety, consistency, and guest experience.

2. Student Pathways

  • Advanced Adventurer (AA): 5 adventure dives from different specialties. Required: Navigation + Deep (where conditions allow). Remaining 3 chosen based on conditions and student interest (e.g., Perfect Buoyancy, Night/Limited Visibility, Boat, Waves/Tides/Currents, Photo/Video, Search & Recovery).
  • Advanced Open Water (AOW equivalent): Follow the same dive themes; complete associated academic modules as per agency requirements.
  • Partial / Fewer than 5 dives: Issue recognition of training for completed dives; student can return to finish remaining dives later.

3. Prerequisites

  • Open Water Diver certification (or equivalent)
  • Recent dive within 6 months recommended; otherwise a short refresher may be conducted
  • Minimum age per SSI standards for selected dives (e.g., Deep)

5. Paperwork & Eligibility

Important: All paperwork must be verified before any water entry (confined or open water).
  1. Responsible Diver Code signed
  2. Medical Declaration reviewed; doctor’s clearance if required
  3. Liability Waiver & Hold Harmless signed
  4. Student details entered in Dive India training register & SSI app
  5. For Night/Deep/Navigation: confirm age, prerequisites, and equipment (torch, compass, computer)

7. Site Selection Guidelines

  • Nemo Reef: Default for Navigation / Buoyancy and sheltered training
  • Boat sites: Choose based on current, visibility, and student comfort; Deep only when conditions are favourable
  • Instructor discretion is final; safety and learning take precedence over finishing all five dives on a fixed timeline

8. SSI ABC Buddy Check

  • A – Air delivery system: Reg setup, SPG pressure, alternate, hose routing; breathe from both primary & alternate
  • B – Buoyancy control system: Include weights; test inflate/deflate; LPI connection; dumps operational; weights fitted & releasable
  • C – Clips: Secure dangling equipment to prevent entanglement or reef contact

9. Certification Decision & Documentation

  • Record completed themes and academic sign‑offs; log dives properly
  • If fewer than 5 dives completed: issue recognition for completed dives and brief on return options
  • Submit certification once all requirements met; brief on depth limits and recommended experience

10. Instructor Reminders

  • Use standard Dive India briefings; document any deviations and reasons (weather, illness, logistics)
  • Ensure torches, compasses, SMB/DSMB, and computers are available when relevant
  • Paperwork, safety, and training quality take priority over schedule pressure
